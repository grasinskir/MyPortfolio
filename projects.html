Projects

Pong

Pong was the first game I ever programmed. I learned how to manipulate variables and functions in order to accomplish a certain task. I learned how to keep score, make a ball bounce within a certain area, and even set up a play button. I also learned how to count lives and make an object (the ball in this case) change velocity.

Star Destroyer

Star Destroyer was my next big project after Pong. Here I learned how to incorporate arrays into my code and also multiple objects at once. I also learned how to add pictures and sound to my game, adding a spaceship aliens, and a background. I later recoded this game to incorporate object oriented programming with Javascript. I turned the bullets and aliens into objects. If you play both games in the links below you will notice that the games run entirely the same (like they should).

Flappy Bison

Flappy Bison is my spin off of the once popular "Flappy Bird". I learned physics, the acceleration of the bison, to my program. I used completely object oriented programming for this game. I learned how to also animate the tail of the bison by using the setInterval function. I also incorporated a scrolling background.

Master of Pie

Master of Pie is my take on the arcade game Asteroids. I learned how to move an object across a screen in two dimensions and how to account for both the collisions of bullets and the space rocks. I implemented the idea of the space rocks splitting in two as they are destroyed. I also created the game so that it could be played either on a mobile device or a computer. The game features instructions and even a control panel just for mobile mode.
